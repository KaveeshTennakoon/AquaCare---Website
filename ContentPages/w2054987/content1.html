Saving Corals and Reducing Ocean Acidification

Saving corals and reducing ocean acidification may greatly affect the biodiversity in marine waters. However, this will not just affect the marine area but is also vital for land dwellers, including us humans. This webpage may contribute towards your understanding of the benefits of saving corals and reducing ocean acidification, including the activities that can be taken towards the ultimate goal of protecting marine habitats.

Saving Corals

Why Saving Corals so Important?

  • Supports Biodiversity by providing habitats for numerous marine species
  • Protects coastlines from erosion by forming barriers that reduce the wave energy
  • Contributes towards nutrient cycling and water filtration, providing economic benefits to coastal communities

Actions Towards Saving Corals

  • Reducing Carbon Emissions to Mitigate Climate Change
  • Promoting Sustainable Fishing Practices
  • Reducing Overfishing may benefit the ecosystem of coral reefs
  • Implementing Marine protected areas and enforcing regulations to prevent destructive activities that may destroy coral habitats
    1. Dynamite Fishing
    2. Coral Mining
  • Raising awareness and educating communities about the importance of coral reefs You can see several organizations that contribute solely to this cause

Reducing Ocean Acidification

Ocean Acidification? What is that?

Occurs when carbon dioxide (CO2) from the atmosphere is absorbed by seawater, which leads to a decrease in the ocean's pH levels, making the seawater more acidic.

Why is It Harmful?

  • Interferes with the ability of marine organisms like corals, shellfish, and some plankton species to build their shells leading to reduced growth rates and weakened structures
  • Disrupts marine food webs affecting survival and reproduction of many species
  • Impacts the availability of carbonate ions, essential for calcifying organisms, and alter ocean chemistry, potentially affecting nutrient cycling and biodiversity
  • Ocean acidification indirectly affects fisheries and tourism

Activities Towards Reducing Ocean Acidification

  • Transitioning to renewable energy sources
  • Promoting energy efficiency
  • Implementing policies to limit the carbon emissions from industries and transportation
  • Protecting and restoring coastal ecosystems
  • Raising awareness and advocating for international cooperation to address climate change ocean acidification on a global scale
